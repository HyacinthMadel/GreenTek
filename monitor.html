Climate Action: Monitor

Monitoring is the first and most important step in climate action. It involves regularly observing and collecting information about our environment, such as temperature changes, air and water quality, carbon emissions, energy usage, and waste production. By keeping track of these indicators, we can clearly see how human activities affect the planet. Monitoring helps us identify early warning signs of environmental problems, like rising pollution levels or unusual weather patterns. With accurate data, communities, schools, and governments can make better decisions, create effective solutions, and measure whether their actions are making a positive impact. In short, monitoring helps us understand what is happening now, so we can plan wisely for a healthier and more sustainable future.
